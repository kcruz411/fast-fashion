How Fast Fashion Affects Workers

Many companies, such as Forever 21, H&M, and Nordstrom's, use workers living in poverty in third-world countries, 80% of whom are women, to work as their slaves. They churn out clothes all day long for less than $1.00 every day. These women and children often have no other choice but to work in these factories, because they need to find a way to provide for their families.

Because the workers at these factories are desperate to provide for their families, workplace abuse is not considered as something important for factories to fix. In fact, the short-term contracts these factories use include a high risk for termination, especially if workers speak out against their superiors' or coworkers' abuse. Even if the woman speaks out against her abusers, she will become blacklisted at all the factories, and she won't be able to find work anywhere. Workers are pushed too hard, constantly working through their breaks and overtime with little or no pay. When they fail to meet these targets, workers may experience physical, verbal, and sexual violence as punishments.
